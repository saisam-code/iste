About ISTE

The Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE) is the leading national professional non-profit making Society for the Technical Education System in our country.

The Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE) is a national, professional, non-profit making Society registered under the Societies Registration Act of 1860.

ISTE students' chapter at our college aims to provide technical awareness to students and enhance their skills through various workshops, seminars, and competitions. We strive to bridge the gap between academia and industry.

ISTE has over 1.5 lakh individual members and more than 3,500 institutional members including IITs, NITs, and other leading technical institutions across India.

Our mission is to assist and contribute to the production and development of top-quality professional engineers and technicians needed by the industries and other organizations.

Key objectives:

  • To provide quality training programs to teachers and administrators of technical institutions
  • To help technical institutions in curriculum development
  • To promote research and development in technical education
  • To organize technical events and competitions for students

Our vision is to be the leading organization for technical education that fosters innovation, excellence, and leadership in engineering education and research.

We envision:

  • A technical education system that produces globally competent engineers
  • An ecosystem that promotes industry-academia collaboration
  • Technical institutions that are centers of excellence in teaching and research
  • Students who are innovators and problem solvers

The Indian Society for Technical Education was started in 1941 as the Association of Principals of Technical Institutions (APTI). It was converted into "Indian Society for Technical Education" in 1968 with a view to enlarge its activities to advance the cause of technological education.

Major milestones:

  • 1941: Founded as APTI
  • 1968: Renamed as ISTE
  • 1981: Granted recognition by AICTE
  • 2005: Launched ISTE National Student Chapters
  • 2020: Reached 3,500+ institutional members
